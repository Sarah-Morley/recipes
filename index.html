RECIPE: White Button Mushrooms on Toast

Lemony and garlicky, these sauteed button-mushrooms with fresh herbs are an easy side for any season

I first made this recipe of white button mushrooms on toast using sourdough rye bread, but any thinly-sliced, dense bread should be able to soak up the pan juices without disintegrating. Adding the fresh curly parsley just before serving adds a bright aroma and lively color to the plate. --sm

Ingredients

  • 1Lb button mushrooms, halved
  • 1/8 c olive oil
  • 2 small garlic cloves, minced
  • salt and pepper
  • 1 half-lemon, juiced
  • 2 slices bread, for toasting
  • 4 Tbsp chopped fresh parsley

Preparation

  1. Put all ingredients except bread into a medium size bowl and toss gently to distribute oil, lemon juice and minced garlic among the mushrooms. Let mushrooms marinate for 10 minutes, tossing occasionally.

  2. Place a wide skillet over medium heat.

  3. Add mushrooms and cook, stirring, until lightly browned, 6 to 8 minutes.

  4. Toast bread to golden brown and place one piece in a shallow bowl.

  5. Remove mushrooms from heat and add in fresh herbs, toss briefly and spoon onto toast, drizzling a bit of pan juices on each serving.
